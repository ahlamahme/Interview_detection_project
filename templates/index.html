What is ARI?

ARI(Artificial Resources Interview) is a website designed by 4th year students in Ain Shams University. This website is our graduation project.

Mission

To improve interviewee non-verbal skills to guarantee the best impression in a job interview.

Vision

Provide the most accurate analysis of the interviewee’s non-verbal communication skills and give out tips for improvement.

What do we provide?

Through our chatbot you can start a mock interview session and get some interview tips by clicking on "Interview Tips" in the tab above.

  • Exposure to real interview questions

    When you start the mock interview session, you will be asked the most common questions asked during real job interviews. This will give you a sneak peek on what you could be asked about during an interview.

  • Feedback on your performance

    After the session ends, you will be able to download a full report that contains analysis on how well you performed during the mock session. Also, you can download the video for the whole session so you can see where things went wrong.

  • Tips & tricks to get better

    Inside the report, you will find tips to enhance your performance in interviews. These tips are based on the weak points discovered during the mock interview.

Research the company

Understanding key information about the company you’re interviewing with can help you go into your interview with confidence. Using the company’s website, social media posts and recent press releases will provide a solid understanding of the company’s goals.

Read More
Practice your answers

Prepare your answer to the common question: “Tell me about yourself, and why are you interested in this role with our company?” The idea is to quickly communicate who you are and what value you will bring to the company and the role—it’s your elevator pitch.

Read More
Use the STAR method

Prepare to be asked about times in the past when you used a specific skill and use the STAR method to tell stories with a clear Situation, Task, Action and Result. Follow all parts of the STAR method. Be specific at all times, without rambling or including too much information.

More Detail
Dress Professionally

Remember, nothing too flashy or suggestive with clothing. Be on the safe side with clothes. Do research on the general dress code for the industry you're interviewing in. For most, something along the lines of business casual will work.

Read More
Remember your nonverbals

During a job interview, the way you present yourself is just as important as what you say. Body language is an important indicator of your comfort, confidence and interest for interviewers. Be aware of your body language to give the best impression possible.

Discover
Be early

If your interview is offline, you should map out your route to the interview location so you can be sure to arrive on time. If you’re taking public transportation, identify a backup plan if there are delays or closures. If your interview is online then, login 15-20 minutes earlier and make sure that you have a proper background and that everything works well. You can hang out until your scheduled time.

Follow-up afterwards

Showing your interest and dedication can go a long way in convincing a hiring manager you are the right person for the job. While you don’t want to be aggressive, desperate or obnoxious, you don’t want to come off as passive, apathetic or lackadaisical either! Before the interview is over, ask when they will likely get back to you. Always follow up with a brief thank you a few days after the interview.
